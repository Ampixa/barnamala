\section{Method}

\subsection{Compact Student Architecture}

Our student network, \modelname{}, is a pre-activation squeeze-and-excitation
residual network~\cite{he2016identity,hu2018senet} designed to operate on
single-channel $32{\times}32$ images.  The architecture consists of a $3{\times}3$
stem convolution followed by three stages of PreAct SE blocks with channel widths
$(40, 80, 160)$ and depths $(2, 2, 2)$.  Each stage except the first downsamples
spatially by a factor of two via a strided convolution in the first block.  Within
each block, a standard squeeze-and-excitation module (reduction ratio 8) recalibrates
channel responses after the second convolution.  Global average pooling (GAP)
collapses the spatial dimensions, and a single dropout layer ($p = 0.1$) precedes
the linear classifier, which maps to 46 classes (the DHCD character inventory).
The total trainable parameter count is \textbf{1,109,116}.

\subsection{Teacher Ensemble}

We train an ensemble of 15 teachers by crossing three architecture configurations
with five independent random seeds (seeds 0--4).  Two configurations share widths
$(96, 192, 384)$ with depths $(3, 3, 3)$ but differ in data-augmentation
intensity (medium vs.\ heavy tier; see Section~\ref{sec:training}); a third uses
widths $(64, 128, 256)$ with depths $(4, 4, 4)$ and medium augmentation.
This yields parameter counts of 5.97\,M and 9.90\,M per teacher respectively,
spanning a 5.97--9.90\,M range across the ensemble.
All teachers use the same \modelname{} codebase (the \texttt{DevNet} class) and
are trained without distillation; their logits are dumped once and stored in a
single NumPy archive that is reused across student runs.

\subsection{Ensemble Knowledge Distillation}
\label{sec:kd}

We follow the knowledge distillation framework of Hinton et al.\
\cite{hinton2015distill}.  For each training image the ensemble logit is the
mean of the 15 teacher logits computed at inference time on the raw (no
test-time augmentation) input.  The distillation loss combines a
temperature-softened KL divergence with a hard cross-entropy term:
\begin{equation}
  \mathcal{L} = \alpha \cdot T^{2}\,
    \mathrm{KL}\!\left(
      \sigma\!\left(\tfrac{z_{s}}{T}\right) \,\Big\|\,
      \sigma\!\left(\tfrac{z_{t}}{T}\right)
    \right)
    + (1-\alpha)\cdot\mathrm{CE}(z_{s}, y),
  \label{eq:kdloss}
\end{equation}
where $z_{s}$ and $z_{t}$ are student and (mean) teacher logits, $\sigma$
denotes the softmax, $T = 4.0$ is the temperature, and $\alpha = 0.7$ weights
the soft target relative to the hard label.  The label-smoothing coefficient
($\varepsilon = 0.1$) is applied to the hard cross-entropy term.
Teacher logits are pre-computed without any test-time augmentation so that they
represent clean, unbiased soft targets.

\subsection{Script-Aware Training}
\label{sec:training}

Devanagari script is not horizontally symmetric: reflecting a glyph produces an
invalid character.  We therefore \emph{exclude} horizontal flips entirely from
the augmentation pipeline.  The training transform instead applies affine
perturbations (rotation, translation, scale, shear) followed by elastic
deformation; the intensity is controlled by an augmentation tier
(\texttt{light}/\texttt{medium}/\texttt{heavy}) selected per configuration.
Random erasing ($p = 0.25$, area 2--10\%) provides additional regularisation.

At the batch level we apply mixup~\cite{zhang2018mixup} ($\alpha = 0.2$) and
CutMix~\cite{yun2019cutmix} ($\alpha = 1.0$) with equal probability, each
activated with overall probability 0.5 per batch.  Mixing is disabled during
distillation runs because per-image teacher logits cannot be linearly combined
without introducing a bias in the soft-target distribution; the KD loss
(Equation~\ref{eq:kdloss}) uses clean, unaugmented teacher predictions for every
training sample.

Optimisation uses AdamW with a cosine learning-rate schedule preceded by a
5-epoch linear warm-up.  Weight exponential moving average (EMA, decay 0.999)
tracks a shadow copy of the model; the EMA weights are used for validation and
saved as the final checkpoint.

\subsection{Evaluation Protocol}

All reported accuracies are obtained from a single forward pass over the DHCD
held-out test split; no test-time augmentation is used.  Model selection during
training relies on a 10\% stratified-held-out validation set carved from the
official training split before any augmentation, ensuring class balance is
preserved throughout.

Statistical comparisons between \modelname{} and the MallaNet
baseline~\cite{malla2025} use the exact two-sided McNemar
test~\cite{mcnemar1947} applied to per-sample binary correctness indicators on
the same test set.  Confidence intervals for accuracy and error rate are Wilson
score intervals at 95\%~\cite{wilson1927}.  Calibration quality is measured by
Expected Calibration Error (ECE)~\cite{guo2017calibration} computed with 15
equal-width bins on the held-out test set.
