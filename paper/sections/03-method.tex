\section{Method}

\subsection{Compact Student Architecture}

\modelname{} is a pre-activation squeeze-and-excitation residual network ~\cite{he2016identity,hu2018senet} designed for single-channel $32{\times}32$ images. The channel width is $(40, 80, 160)$ over three depths $(2, 2, 2)$. Stages ~2 and ~3 are downsampled by stride ~2 (Figures ~\ref{fig:arch}). The total number of learnable parameters is \textbf{1,109,116}.

\subsection{Teacher Ensemble}

The ensemble consists of 15 teachers drawn from three architectural configurations intersected with five independent random seeds (seeds 0 to 4). Two of these configurations share width $(96, 192, 384)$ and depth $(3, 3, 3)$ but differ in expansion strength (intermediate and overlying, see sections ~ \ref{sec:training}). The third uses width $(64, 128, 256)$ and depth $(4, 4, 4)$ with medium expansion. The number of parameters is 5.97\,M and 9.89\,M. The scattering throughout the setting is intentional to reduce mode collapse in soft targets. All teachers share the same \modelname{} codebase (\texttt{DevNet} classes) and are trained without extraction. Their logs are dumped once into a single NumPy archive and reused across all student runs.

\subsection{Ensemble Knowledge Distillation}
\label{sec:kd}

Following Hinton et al. ~\cite{hinton2015distill}, the ensemble target for each training image is the average softmax of nine supervised checkpoints (configurations A, B, C $\times$ seeds 0 to 2), and each supervised softmax incorporates the average of horizontal flip test times. The purpose is to:\begin{equation}
  \mathcal{L} = \alpha \cdot T^{2}\,
    \mathrm{KL}\!\left(
      \sigma\!\left(\tfrac{z_{t}}{T}\right) \,\Big\|\,
      \sigma\!\left(\tfrac{z_{s}}{T}\right)
    \right)
    + (1-\alpha)\cdot\mathrm{CE}(z_{s}, y),
  \label{eq:kdloss}
\end{equation}Here, $z_{s}$ and $z_{t}$ are the student and (average) teacher logits, $\sigma$ is the softmax, $T = 4.0$, and $\alpha = 0.7$. Label smoothing ($\varepsilon = 0.1$) is applied to hard cross entropy terms. KL is teacher-to-student (teacher as reference distribution). Target generation scheme (no clean TTA vs. flipped average) and ensemble size (9 teachers vs. 15 teachers) are removed in sections ~\ref{sec:ablations}.

\subsection{Script-Aware Training}
\label{sec:training}

Devanagari Script is not horizontally symmetric. Reflecting glyphs produces invalid characters. Horizontal flips are completely excluded. Instead, the training transformation applies affine perturbations (rotation, translation, scale, shear) followed by elastic deformations. The strength is controlled by the selected enhancement layer (\texttt{light}/\texttt{medium}/\texttt{heavy}) for each configuration. Random purge ($p = 0.25$, area 2--10\%) completes image-wise normalization.

The mixing extensions, mixup~\cite{zhang2018mixup} ($\alpha = 0.2$) and CutMix~\cite{yun2019cutmix} ($\alpha = 1.0$), are selected with equal probability and each is activated for each batch with an overall probability of 0.5. Both are turned off while distillation is running. The KD loss (formula~\ref{eq:kdloss}) always shows a clean, unenhanced teacher prediction, because the per-image teacher logits cannot be linearly combined without compromising the soft target distribution.

Use AdamW for optimization. The learning rate schedule is cosine with a linear warmup of 5 epochs. A weighted exponential moving average (EMA, decay 0.999) tracks shadow copies. EMA weights are used for validation and final checkpoint.

\subsection{Evaluation Protocol}

Reported accuracy is from a single forward pass on the DHCD holdout test split. test-time augmentation is not applicable. Model selection relies on a 10% stratified validation set cut from the pre-augmentation training split.

For statistical comparisons between \modelname{} and the baseline ~\cite{malla2025}, the exact two-sided per-sample binary accuracy index McNemar test~\cite{mcnemar1947} is used. Confidence intervals for accuracy and error rate are Wilson score intervals at 95\%~\cite{wilson1927}. ECE~\cite{guo2017calibration} is computed using 15 equal width bins on the held-out test set.
