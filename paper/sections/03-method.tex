\section{Method}

\subsection{Compact Student Architecture}

Our student network, \modelname{}, is a pre-activation squeeze-and-excitation
residual network~\cite{he2016identity,hu2018senet} for single-channel
$32{\times}32$ images.  A $3{\times}3$ stem convolution feeds three stages of
PreAct SE blocks with channel widths $(40, 80, 160)$ and depths $(2, 2, 2)$.
Each stage except the first downsamples spatially by a factor of two using a
strided convolution in the first block.  A standard squeeze-and-excitation module
(reduction ratio 8) recalibrates channel responses after the second convolution
in each block.  Global average pooling (GAP) collapses the spatial dimensions;
a single dropout layer ($p = 0.1$) then precedes the linear classifier, which
maps to 46 classes (the DHCD character inventory).
The total trainable parameter count is \textbf{1,109,116}.

\subsection{Teacher Ensemble}

We train an ensemble of 15 teachers from three architecture configurations and
five independent random seeds (seeds 0--4).  Two
configurations share widths $(96, 192, 384)$ with depths $(3, 3, 3)$ but use
different data-augmentation intensities (medium vs.\ heavy tier; see
Section~\ref{sec:training}); the third uses widths $(64, 128, 256)$ with depths
$(4, 4, 4)$ and medium augmentation.  These choices yield parameter counts of
5.97\,M and 9.90\,M per teacher respectively, spanning a 5.97--9.90\,M range
across the ensemble.  All teachers use the same \modelname{} codebase (the
\texttt{DevNet} class) and are trained without distillation.  Their logits are
dumped once and stored in a single NumPy archive reused across student runs.

\subsection{Ensemble Knowledge Distillation}
\label{sec:kd}

We follow the knowledge distillation framework of Hinton et al.\
\cite{hinton2015distill}.  For each training image, the ensemble logit is the
mean of the 15 teacher logits computed at inference time on the raw input, with
no test-time augmentation.  The distillation objective combines a
temperature-softened KL divergence and a hard cross-entropy term:
\begin{equation}
  \mathcal{L} = \alpha \cdot T^{2}\,
    \mathrm{KL}\!\left(
      \sigma\!\left(\tfrac{z_{s}}{T}\right) \,\Big\|\,
      \sigma\!\left(\tfrac{z_{t}}{T}\right)
    \right)
    + (1-\alpha)\cdot\mathrm{CE}(z_{s}, y),
  \label{eq:kdloss}
\end{equation}
where $z_{s}$ and $z_{t}$ are student and (mean) teacher logits, $\sigma$
denotes the softmax, $T = 4.0$ is the temperature, and $\alpha = 0.7$ weights
the soft target relative to the hard label.  We apply the label-smoothing
coefficient ($\varepsilon = 0.1$) to the hard cross-entropy term.  Because the
teacher logits are pre-computed without any test-time augmentation, they
represent clean, unbiased soft targets.

\subsection{Script-Aware Training}
\label{sec:training}

Devanagari script is not horizontally symmetric: reflecting a glyph produces an
invalid character.  For this reason, we \emph{exclude} horizontal flips entirely
from the augmentation pipeline.  The training transform applies affine perturbations
(rotation, translation, scale, shear) followed by elastic deformation; an
augmentation tier (\texttt{light}/\texttt{medium}/\texttt{heavy}) selected per
configuration controls the intensity.
Random erasing ($p = 0.25$, area 2--10\%) provides additional regularisation.

At the batch level, mixup~\cite{zhang2018mixup} ($\alpha = 0.2$) and
CutMix~\cite{yun2019cutmix} ($\alpha = 1.0$) are selected with equal
probability, each activated with overall probability 0.5 per batch.  We disable
mixing during distillation runs because per-image teacher logits cannot be
linearly combined without introducing bias in the soft-target distribution; the
KD loss (Equation~\ref{eq:kdloss}) uses clean, unaugmented teacher predictions
for every training sample.

Optimisation uses AdamW with a cosine learning-rate schedule preceded by a
5-epoch linear warm-up.  A weight exponential moving average (EMA, decay 0.999)
tracks a shadow copy of the model; validation and the final checkpoint use the
EMA weights.

\subsection{Evaluation Protocol}

All reported accuracies come from a single forward pass over the DHCD held-out
test split; no test-time augmentation is used.  For model selection during
training, we use a 10\% stratified-held-out validation set carved from the
official training split before any augmentation, preserving class balance
throughout.

Statistical comparisons between \modelname{} and the MallaNet
baseline~\cite{malla2025} use the exact two-sided McNemar
test~\cite{mcnemar1947} on per-sample binary correctness indicators from the
same test set.  Confidence intervals for accuracy and error rate are Wilson
score intervals at 95\%~\cite{wilson1927}.  We measure calibration quality with
Expected Calibration Error (ECE)~\cite{guo2017calibration}, computed with 15
equal-width bins on the held-out test set.
