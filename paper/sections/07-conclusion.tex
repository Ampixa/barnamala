\section{Conclusion}
\label{sec:conclusion}

This paper demonstrates that a \textbf{1.11\,M}-parameter compact student,
\modelname{}, matches the 17.32\,M-parameter MallaNet~\cite{malla2025}
at statistical parity on DHCD (McNemar $p = 0.345$, C1), and that this
parity holds even without knowledge distillation.  Rigorous paired
evaluation (C2) reveals an 11-image intrinsic floor shared by every
model tested — including large teacher ensembles — identifying DHCD as a
saturated benchmark rather than a task with room for meaningful
architectural progress at current resolution.  Ablations (C3) confirm
that no lever — distillation target cleanliness, ensemble size, or TTA —
produces a statistically significant gain; efficiency profiling (C4)
shows a 15.6$\times$ size and latency advantage that comes at no accuracy
cost within the saturation regime; and full reproducible artifacts are
released (C5).

Compact models are not merely adequate at saturation; they are the
appropriate tool: given that error reduction is bounded by label noise
and handwriting ambiguity rather than model capacity, deploying a model
that is an order of magnitude smaller and faster carries no penalty.

Future work should prioritise two directions: first, \emph{paired
evaluation protocols} (McNemar or similar) as a community standard,
so that accuracy gains of a few hundredths of a percent are not
reported without a significance test; and second, \emph{fresh,
harder benchmarks} for Devanagari — higher-resolution scans, genuinely
independent writer pools, or multi-script character inventories — that
do not share DHCD's provenance and can support discrimination between
models that DHCD can no longer distinguish.
