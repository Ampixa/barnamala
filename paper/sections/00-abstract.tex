\begin{abstract}
We present \modelname{}, a compact convolutional student network with
\textbf{1.11\,M} parameters that achieves \textbf{99.73\%} accuracy on the
DHCD Devanagari handwriting benchmark~\cite{acharya2015dhcd}---statistically
indistinguishable (McNemar \(p=0.345\)) from the \textbf{17.32\,M}-parameter
MallaNet~\cite{malla2025} at \textbf{15.6\(\times\)} smaller, and it ties
MallaNet \emph{even without knowledge distillation}.
Applying a rigorous paired evaluation protocol (exact McNemar tests with
Wilson confidence intervals) across all student--teacher configurations, we
find that \emph{no} configuration achieves a statistically significant win:
DHCD sits at an \textbf{11-error intrinsic floor} shared by every model tested,
indicating the benchmark is \textbf{saturated}.
Beyond DHCD, \modelname{} transfers to CMATERdb digit recognition
(zero-shot \textbf{76.6\%}; after light fine-tuning \textbf{97.8\%}) and
proves substantially more robust to blur and contrast corruptions than
MallaNet (overall mean corruption accuracy \textbf{75.7\%} vs.\
\textbf{38.7\%}).
All code, model weights, and experimental artifacts are available at~\repourl.
\end{abstract}
