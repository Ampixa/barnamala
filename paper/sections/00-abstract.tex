\begin{abstract}
\modelname{} is a compact convolutional network (\textbf{1.11\,M} parameters)
reaching \textbf{99.73\,\%} top-1 accuracy on the 46-class DHCD Devanagari
benchmark~\cite{acharya2015dhcd}---the highest reported, at \textbf{15.6\(\times\)}
smaller footprint than prior state-of-the-art. The catch: every model
tested---large teacher ensembles included---hits the same
\textbf{11-error intrinsic floor}. No configuration achieves a statistically
clear win under exact McNemar tests with Wilson confidence intervals.
DHCD is \textbf{saturated}.
Even without knowledge distillation, the student matches the nearest
large-model baseline (17.32\,M parameters; McNemar \(p=0.345\)).
Outside DHCD the picture changes: \modelname{} reaches \textbf{76.6\,\%}
zero-shot on CMATERdb digits (\textbf{97.8\,\%} after fine-tuning) and
survives image corruptions far better than large baselines do
(mean corruption accuracy \textbf{75.7\,\%} vs.\ \textbf{38.7\,\%}).
All artifacts are at~\repourl.
\end{abstract}
