\begin{abstract}
\modelname{} is a compact convolutional network (\textbf{1.11\,M} parameters)
reaching \textbf{99.73\,\%} top-1 accuracy on the 46-class DHCD Devanagari
benchmark~\cite{acharya2015dhcd}---the highest reported result, at a
\textbf{15.6\(\times\)} smaller footprint than prior state-of-the-art.
Exact McNemar tests with Wilson confidence intervals, applied across every
student--teacher pairing, found that \emph{no} configuration achieves a
statistically significant win: every model tested---large teacher ensembles
included---converges to the same \textbf{11-error intrinsic floor}, indicating
DHCD is \textbf{saturated} at current resolution.
Somewhat surprisingly, parity with the nearest large-model baseline
(17.32\,M parameters; McNemar \(p=0.345\)) holds \emph{even without
knowledge distillation}.
Outside DHCD, \modelname{} transfers to CMATERdb digit recognition
(zero-shot \textbf{76.6\,\%}; fine-tuned \textbf{97.8\,\%}) and is
substantially more robust to common image corruptions than large baselines
(mean corruption accuracy \textbf{75.7\,\%} vs.\ \textbf{38.7\,\%}).
All code, weights, and artifacts are at~\repourl.
\end{abstract}
