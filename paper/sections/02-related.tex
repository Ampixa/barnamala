\section{Related Work}
\label{sec:related}

\paragraph{Indic and Devanagari handwritten character recognition.}
The DHCD dataset~\cite{acharya2015dhcd} has been the standard 46-class benchmark
since 2015. MallaNet~\cite{malla2025} couples residual branch merging with
homogeneous filter capsule (HFC) layers to reach 99.71\,\% at 17.32\,M
parameters; Mishra et al.~\cite{mishra2021} independently report 99.72\,\% with
a comparably large architecture---for practical purposes, indistinguishable.
A concurrent preprint reaches 99.80\,\% on the 10-class digit subset~\cite{malla2025digit},
though digit-only results are not comparable to the 46-class task addressed here.

\paragraph{Knowledge distillation and compact model design.}
Hinton et al.~\cite{hinton2015distill} showed that soft probability
outputs---``dark knowledge''---from a large teacher can guide a smaller student
to match teacher accuracy at substantially lower capacity.
Residual identity mappings~\cite{he2016identity} and squeeze-and-excitation
channel re-weighting~\cite{hu2018senet} are standard building blocks for compact
architectures that retain representational power while cutting parameter counts.
To our knowledge, knowledge distillation has not previously been applied to the
46-class DHCD benchmark, and has rarely been stress-tested on saturated
benchmarks where the performance gap is already buried inside sampling variation.

\paragraph{Statistical significance and benchmark saturation.}
When top models err on fewer than 0.3\,\% of examples, raw accuracy rankings
can shift run-to-run without any real difference in model quality.
McNemar's test~\cite{mcnemar1947} is the appropriate paired framework:
conditioning on disagreement examples, it stays sensitive to genuine
error-pattern differences while remaining robust to the high base-rate of
shared correct predictions.
Wilson score intervals~\cite{wilson1927} provide calibrated single-model
accuracy bounds; calibration analysis~\cite{guo2017calibration} connects
confidence to empirical accuracy, a prerequisite for interpreting soft
distillation targets.
The prior work~\cite{malla2025} applies McNemar against four weaker baselines
but omits its nearest competitor (Mishra et al., 99.72\,\%) and reports no exact $p$-values.
\modelname{} closes this gap by reporting exact McNemar $p$-values and Wilson
CIs across every comparison and showing the benchmark is saturated.
