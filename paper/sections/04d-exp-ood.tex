\subsection{Cross-Dataset Generalisation}
\label{sec:ood}

Both models are evaluated zero-shot on two independently collected Devanagari datasets---separate writers and scanner workflows, each from a different collection year---with no fine-tuning applied.

\paragraph{NHCD (Pant 2012).}
The Nepali Handwritten Character Dataset~\cite{pant2012nhcd} covers the same 46-class inventory in $28{\times}28$ JPEG images; we polarity-invert and bilinearly resize to $32{\times}32$ before evaluation.
\modelname{} achieves \textbf{78.92\%} zero-shot
(95.80\% on digits, 72.33\% on consonants).
The baseline~\cite{malla2025} collapses to \textbf{23.99\%}
(58.85\% on digits, 10.38\% on consonants)---a \textbf{55\,pp gap} despite
identical in-distribution performance on DHCD, with the +62\,pp consonant
disparity being the most striking number.

The dental/retroflex pairs are the clearest failure mode. Each of the four pairs
(ta/\d{t}a, tha/\d{t}ha, da/\d{d}a, dha/\d{d}ha) collapses to near-zero accuracy,
driven by cross-class confusion between strokes whose rendering conventions differ
between Indian and Nepali handwriting traditions---a script-level domain gap neither
model resolves. Outside this cluster, \modelname{} and the baseline diverge sharply.

\paragraph{Prashanth et al.\ 2021.}
After $32{\times}32$ resize, \modelname{} achieves \textbf{79.31\%} zero-shot on
this 22{,}500-image digit dataset~\cite{prashanth2021}; the baseline achieves \textbf{72.60\%}.
These numbers track the NHCD digit outcome and the CMATERdb result from Section~\ref{sec:transfer}.
Digit~5 confusing with digit~7 at high rates is a genuine visual similarity in the Devanagari script, not a training artifact.

Matching in-distribution accuracy conceals where the gap is. Both out-of-distribution evaluations
favour \modelname{}---as does the corruption result in Section~\ref{sec:robustness}. This
pattern across three held-out evaluations suggests that distilling from a diverse teacher
ensemble transfers better across writer and scanner variation than the raw DHCD score implies.
